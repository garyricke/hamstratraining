Brand Story

Four Decades of Earned Trust

Founded in 1978 and headquartered in Frankfort, Illinois, Hamstra Roofing is a second-generation, family-owned company built on the belief that honest work, deep craft knowledge, and genuine care for the customer are the foundation of a lasting business.

No Salesmen — Diagnostic Experts

Every estimator is a trained roofer first. No commissions. No pressure. Just honest assessments of what your roof actually needs.

Vertically Integrated Operations

In-house sheet metal fabrication, bulk shingle warehousing, and a dedicated service department — full quality control from material to installation.

Community & Christian Values

Hamstra regularly donates labor and expertise to local ministries and charitable organizations, treating stewardship as a core business responsibility.

Longevity as Sustainability

"Making roofs last longer is the most green thing you can do." Hamstra invests in materials and techniques that extend roof life rather than accelerate replacement cycles.

Brand Colors

Color System

The Hamstra palette is built on five core colors. Hamstra Red is the brand's defining mark — commanding, confident, and earned. Hamstra Blue provides a clear, authoritative counterpoint used for emphasis and digital UI accents. Use both with intention.

Primary Red

Hamstra Red

HEX #EB1A2C RGB 235, 26, 44 CMYK 0, 89, 81, 8

Primary brand color. CTAs, accents, dividers, icon fills, and headline emphasis.

Brand Blue

Hamstra Blue

HEX #3B82F6 RGB 59, 130, 246 CMYK 76, 47, 0, 4

Digital accent and emphasis color. Course titles, favicon, UI highlights, and emphasis text on dark backgrounds.

Black

Brand Black

HEX #000000 RGB 0, 0, 0 CMYK 0, 0, 0, 100

Dark backgrounds, primary logo version, high-contrast applications.

Charcoal

Hamstra Charcoal

HEX #2F2F2F RGB 47, 47, 47 CMYK 0, 0, 0, 82

Body text, secondary backgrounds, off-black applications. Preferred over pure black for body copy.

Gray

Hamstra Gray

HEX #8A8A8A RGB 138, 138, 138 CMYK 0, 0, 0, 46

Supporting text, metadata, captions, muted UI elements, and the all-gray logo variant.

White

White

HEX #FFFFFF RGB 255, 255, 255 CMYK 0, 0, 0, 0

Page backgrounds, reversed logo applications on dark or red fields, and breathing space.

Digital (Web & App)

  • Red for primary buttons and key links
  • Blue for emphasis text, course titles, and digital UI accents
  • Black/Charcoal for navigation backgrounds
  • Gray for body text on white backgrounds
  • Red underlines or borders as accent elements
  • Never use red as a large background color in UI

Print & Signage

  • Red for job signs, truck graphics, and banners
  • Black wordmark on white or light substrates
  • White logo on black, charcoal, or red backgrounds
  • Minimum 1" clear space around all logo variants
  • Match to Pantone 485 C for red in print production

Photography & Video

  • Prefer natural, high-contrast exterior light
  • Job site imagery should feel honest, not staged
  • Red safety equipment and branded gear photograph well
  • Avoid over-saturated filters — let the work speak
  • Team photos: professional but approachable, outdoors preferred

Logo Variants

Favicon / Icon Mark — Red · Favicons, App Icons, Stamps

Favicon / Icon Mark — Black · Single-Color Print
Favicon / Icon Mark — White · Dark & Colored Backgrounds
Logo Do's
  • Use the SVG master files for all digital applications
  • Maintain a clear space equal to the height of the "H" on all sides
  • Use the black logo on white or light-colored backgrounds
  • Use the white logo on black, charcoal, or red backgrounds
  • Use the all-gray logo for embossing, watermarks, or single-color jobs
  • Use the icon mark (favicon) when horizontal space is limited
  • Scale proportionally — always lock aspect ratio
Logo Don'ts
  • Do not stretch, skew, or distort the logo in any direction
  • Do not recolor the logo outside of approved variants
  • Do not place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or decorative effects
  • Do not use the wordmark without the icon mark in primary settings
  • Do not reduce the horizontal logo below 120px wide in digital contexts
  • Do not recreate or retype the wordmark using a font substitute

Credentials & Certifications

Industry Authority

Hamstra holds the highest available credentials in the industry. These should be featured prominently in all sales and marketing materials — they are a key differentiator in a market rife with unlicensed operators.

Owens Corning Platinum Preferred

The highest contractor tier awarded by Owens Corning — given to fewer than 1% of roofing contractors nationwide. Requires exceptional insurance, reliability, and ongoing technical training. Enables extended manufacturer-backed warranties.

GAF Master Roofing Contractor

GAF's highest designation. Requires proven track record, financial stability, and demonstrated commitment to ongoing installer education. Allows Hamstra to offer GAF's strongest warranty products — unavailable through lesser-certified contractors.

Illinois Unlimited Roofing License

The most comprehensive roofing credential issued by the Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation (IDFPR). Permits work on structures of any size and height. License status: Active. Disciplinary actions: None.

BBB A+ Accreditation

Better Business Bureau A+ rating, accredited since 2007. Reflects consistent delivery of quality work, transparent practices, and effective resolution of any customer concerns. No complaints on record.

Velux Certified Installer

Certified installation partner for Velux skylights and roof windows. Ensures skylight integrations are properly flashed and sealed — a common source of callbacks for uncertified contractors.

GuildQuality Verified

Third-party customer satisfaction surveys via GuildQuality show 100% satisfaction in workmanship quality and problem resolution across 386 surveyed projects. A rare benchmark in the trades.

Messaging Guidance

How to Use Credentials in Copy

Always pair certification names with plain-language benefits. Homeowners don't know what "Platinum Preferred" means on its own — explain that it means extended warranties unavailable from 99% of contractors. The credential earns trust; the explanation earns the call.
